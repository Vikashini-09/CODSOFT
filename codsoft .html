Bhagat Singh - A Revolutionary Hero

Biography

Bhagat Singh (1907â€“1931) was an Indian revolutionary freedom fighter who played a significant role in the Indian independence movement. Born in Banga, Punjab (now in Pakistan), Singh was deeply influenced by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and the prevailing British oppression.

He became a prominent member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA), and was known for his daring actions against the British Raj. He believed in the power of youth and intellect, and wrote extensively on freedom, revolution, and injustice.

Major Contributions

  • Protested the Simon Commission in 1928.
  • Involved in the killing of British officer John Saunders to avenge Lala Lajpat Rai's death.
  • Threw non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929 to protest British laws, and surrendered willingly.
  • Famous for his slogan: "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live the Revolution).

Legacy

Bhagat Singh was hanged on March 23, 1931, at the age of just 23. His sacrifice left an indelible mark on Indian history. Even today, he is remembered as a symbol of courage, patriotism, and youthful rebellion.
